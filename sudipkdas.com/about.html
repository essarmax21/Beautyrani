I am currently working as a postdoc at Ecole Centrale Marseille, France. I obtained Ph.D. from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay working with Prof. Rochish M. Thaokar for the thesis titled "Electrohydrodynamics of Drops, Capsules, and Vesicles".

My research is mainly focused on the computational and theoretical analyses of the deformation of micro-globules such as liquid drop, capsule, and vesicle when subjected to external fluid flow or applied uniform and non-uniform electric fields. Till now, my works were focused on the axisymmetric boundary integral and theoretical analyses of drop and capsule deformation in an applied electric field and external fluid flow. I have published a few articles on drop and capsule deformations, and one manuscript is under review.

For the computational analysis, I follow 3D, axisymmetric, and 2D boundary integral methods and asymptotic theory for the theoretical analysis. I also have a fair understanding of Finite Volume Method (FVM), Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM), Immersed Boundary Method (IBM) for the numerical computation.

I look forward to work on projects involving low-Reynolds number hydrodynamics to enrich my understanding of the dynamics of deformable microglobules. If you have the similar interest, then we can collaborate; please contact me at sudip-kumar.das@univ-amu.fr, sudipkd13@gmail.com.
